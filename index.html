About

Mohadeseh Ghaderian is a passionate undergraduate pursuing a B.Sc. in Biotechnology at the Islamic Azad University (Isfahan, Iran) since September 2023, boasting a GPA of 3.43/4.00. Her diverse research interests span industrial microbiology, microbial biotechnology, agricultural biotechnology and genetic engineering. Mohadeseh is not only academically outstanding but also a visionary leader who translates scientific insights into meaningful applications. Whether designing next‑generation fertilizers, decoding the gut–brain axis or isolating indigenous biofertilizer consortia, she approaches challenges with creativity and perseverance. Beyond the laboratory, she actively mentors students, organizes TEDx programs and teaches cutting‑edge AI courses, embodying a holistic commitment to science and society.

Research Experience

Psychobiotics & the Gut–Brain Axis (Sep 2025 – Present)

  • First author & project lead: spearheaded a pioneering review exploring how the intestinal microbiome shapes mood and mental health via neural, immune and metabolic pathways.
  • Designed a PRISMA‑inspired protocol, implemented a transparent screening and data‑extraction pipeline and coordinated with collaborators to ensure inter‑rater reliability.
  • Synthesized mechanistic evidence on neuroactive metabolites— including GABA, tryptophan derivatives (indoles/kynurenines), short‑chain fatty acids (SCFAs), secondary bile acids, lactate and catecholamines—and linked them to behavioral and clinical endpoints.

Salt‑Mitigating Fertilizer Design (Oct 2024 – Aug 2025)

  • Undergraduate research lead: managed an end‑to‑end agri‑materials project to design fertilizers that reduce soil salinity stress on vegetables and cucurbits, aligning faculty advisors, suppliers and growers.
  • Conducted comprehensive soil diagnostics (electrical conductivity, pH, texture, cation‑exchange capacity and nutrient status) and profiled target crops to translate field constraints into actionable design requirements.
  • Engineered innovative fertilizer formulations that balance ion management and osmotic adjustment strategies while minimizing phytotoxicity and environmental footprint.
  • Developed a lean experimentation pipeline for greenhouse and field trials, implemented quality‑assurance checklists and mentored teammates on safe and reproducible lab practices.
  • Secured competitive funding to transition the project into pilot implementation and earned second place in the Iran National Elites Foundation competition.

Microbiome‑Driven Indigenous Biofertilizer Program (Jan 2024 – Dec 2024)

  • Sole research intern: executed targeted sampling, isolation and functional characterization of soil microbiomes to identify fertility‑enhancing consortia.
  • Established end‑to‑end lab workflows—sampling logistics, aseptic handling, culturing and basic functional assays—with meticulous documentation and quality control.
  • Defined selection criteria around efficacy, stability and safety to advance the strongest strains, supporting the transition from discovery to productization.
  • Results underpin two ongoing M.Sc. theses investigating biofertilizer performance on Iranian medicinal plant seedlings.
